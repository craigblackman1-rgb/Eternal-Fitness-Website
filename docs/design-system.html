✪ Layout

Section Backgrounds

3 treatments

Used for: ApproachSection cards, feature cards (InfographicSystem). Light warm grey with full-contrast text.

Accent/rose background bg-accent — used sparingly

Used for: SpecialiseSection, WhoSection, CTASection (3 sections total). Light rose #C1839F with dark text #4A262E (8.2:1 AA+). Badges use bg-primary/20 text-primary-foreground. CTAs are teal.

✪ Specialist Areas
Dark/teal background bg-primary or bg-hero-overlay

Used for: HeroSection social proof cards, dark sections. White buttons for CTAs. Rose accent for secondary highlights.

Rhythm rule: The live site alternates backgrounds. Rose sections are used sparingly (maximum 3 out of 7–8 sections) to preserve their emphasis. Teal backgrounds are reserved for hero overlays and CTAs only.

Usage rule

Icons only appear inside circular icon frames (56×56px, rounded-full). Used for feature cards, process steps (HowItWorksSection), and hub navigation. Never place icons next to section headings — that is an anti-pattern.

✪ Avoid

Do Not Use

Anti-patterns
Gradient backgrounds
No aggressive purple/violet gradients. Flat colours only — teal, rose, muted, white.
Generic emoji icons
No sparkles, rockets, targets as feature icons. Use SVG system or Lucide.
Left-border accent cards
No rounded cards with a coloured left border. Use tinted backgrounds or elevated cards.
Invented metrics
No "10× faster" or "99.9% uptime" without a source. Honest placeholder beats fake stat.
Filler copy
No "Feature One / Feature Two" or lorem ipsum. Every section must have real copy.
Rose overuse
Limit rose section backgrounds to a maximum of 3 per page. Overuse dilutes the emphasis it provides.
